Osiris, the Egyptian Lord of the Underworld and Judge of the Dead, was once a powerful king known as Usir, a name meaning “mighty”. As the first son of the earth god Geb and sky goddess Nut, he taught humanity the laws of civilization.

His reign was cut short by his brother Set, who—driven by jealousy—trapped Osiris in a cedar-wood chest and cast him into the Nile. Later, Set tore his body into pieces and scattered them across the land. But death could not hold him.

His wife, Isis, recovered the pieces and reassembled them using her magical powers. By breathing life back into him, she established the very first mummy. Though he withdrew from the physical world to rule the realm of the dead, his resurrection promised the hope of eternal life to all.

In the Hall of Judgment, Osiris weighs the hearts of the deceased against the feather of Ma’at, seeking balance rather than perfection. Often depicted with green skin to represent the fertile Nile mud, he symbolizes the cyclical rebirth of grain and the triumph of order over chaos.

With his kind smile and the crook and flail of kingship, Osiris remains the Eternal Lord of rebirth.
