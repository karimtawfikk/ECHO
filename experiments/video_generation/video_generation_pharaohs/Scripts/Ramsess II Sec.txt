Pharaoh Ramesses II, aged twenty, stands before a map of Egypt, pointing to various regions with his staff, surrounded by advisors in traditional Egyptian attire, discussing expansion and trade route security.

In 1258 BCE, Ramesses II stands amidst his troops on the battlefield of Kadesh, surrounded by the remnants of his army, having fought the Hittites to a stalemate, leading to the signing of the world's first known peace treaty.

In the desert, a city rises from the sand, with grand structures and towering temples, including a colossal rock-cut temple featuring four sixty-foot statues of a pharaoh.

Pharaoh Ramesses II, wearing a double crown, stands in the grand temple dedicated to his favorite wife, Nefertari, surrounded by his many sons and daughters, including Merneptah, all wearing traditional Egyptian attire. In the background, the Valley of the Queens can be seen with Nefertari's beautifully decorated tomb visible.

A mummified Ramesses II, dressed in traditional Egyptian attire, lies in a glass case in a dimly lit room within the Egyptian Museum in Cairo, surrounded by various artifacts and hieroglyphics, with a crowd of visitors observing him during a festival of rejuvenation celebration.
